\begin{proof}
    Let $f\geq 0$ be measurable. Consider $E_k=\{2^k<f\leq 2^{k+1}\}$, then $\{f>0\}=\sqcup_{-\infty}^\infty E_k$. Since $f$ is measurable the integration will split over countable disjoint union. Thus
    \[
        \int \abs{f}^p\ d\mu=\int f^p\ d\mu=\int_{\{f>0\}}f^p\ d\mu=\sum_{-\infty}^\infty \int_{E_k}f^p\ d\mu
    \]
    Now suppose $f\in L^p$, then $\lambda_f(\alpha)<\infty$ for $\alpha>0$. Since $\lambda_f(\alpha)=\mu\{\abs{f}>\alpha\}\leq \norm{f}_p^p/\alpha^p<\infty$. Thus $\mu(E_k)=\lambda_f(2^k)-\lambda_f(2^{k+1})$. We get
    \[
        \int\abs{f}^p\ d\mu=\sum_{-\infty}^\infty \int_{E_k}f^p\ d\mu\geq \sum_{-\infty}^\infty 2^{pk}\mu(E_k)=\sum_{-\infty}^\infty 2^{pk}(\lambda_f(2^k)-\lambda_f(2^{k+1}))
    \]
    Thus $\sum_{-\infty}^\infty 2^{pk}(\lambda_f(2^k)-\lambda_f(2^{k+1}))\leq \int\abs{f}^p\ d\mu<\infty$. Thus cancellation is valid and we get,
    \[(1-1/2^p)\sum_{-\infty}^\infty 2\]
\end{proof}
